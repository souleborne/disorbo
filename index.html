About

Joe Disorbo is a writer and director, raised between Singapore and Las Vegas. He attended boarding school in Europe, and obtained his undergraduate degree in a self-made major from Middlebury College in Vermont in 2026.

Growing up steeped in videogames and literature, Disorbo has an interest in marrying popular genre with deeper ideological inquiry; in his work, he grapples with hard literary and philosophical themes across a formidable range of tonal registers, not limited to absurdist comedy, realist horror, political thriller, and war.

Disorbo wrote, directed, produced, and shot his thesis project Envoys, a war-thriller feature film shot on a crowdfunded budget of $9,800. He did the entire postproduction pipeline—editing, visual effects, 3-D, sound, and music composition—as a sole operator, over eighteen months. Envoys will be released free to the general public online Fall 2026, pending festival acceptances.

Disorbo’s previous work includes ROSPO, an independent three-act stage play that he produced when he was 20 years old; MMA (Monday Morning Assembly), a 22-episode comedy news TV show he made in high school; Opinion writing; Music compositions, soundtrack and classical.

Envoys

Feature Film — 65 min — 2026

In the aftermath of a second American civil war, two coalition envoys are sent to a remote rebel encampment to deliver a ceasefire ultimatum to its reclusive, fanatical commander.

Shot on a budget of $9,800, with a completely first-time cast and crew, Envoys is my attempt to raise the bar for what a student film can be. It is the first feature-length student film to be made at Middlebury College, and the first narrative thesis film made completely outside of the college's film department (in protestation of a 15-minute runtime rule).

Rospo

Three-Act Stage Play — 2023

A Catholic missionary and an atheist British aristocrat are stranded on a rowboat in the Atlantic Ocean after their cruise liner sinks — tensions rise until they witness an inexplicable event that shatters both their belief systems.

Written for and produced in the Hepburn Zoo student theater at Middlebury College, Rospo is my theatrical directing debut and my first full-length narrative production. It was an entirely student-organized production — we even built the boat. Rospo sold out all three performances, not in small part from a guerrilla marketing campaign: over a thousand toad stickers hidden around the Middlebury campus… four years later, some still remain.
